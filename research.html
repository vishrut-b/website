Master's Thesis – Observatoire Astronomique de Strasbourg (2024)

It was supervised by Dr. Pierre Ocvirk and Dr. Katarina Kraljic of the Strasbourg Observatory, and my aim was to quantify the impact of inhomogneneous reionization (z = 15 to 5) on the properties of cosmic filaments and haloes. The dataset used came from Cosmic Daw III simulation, which is calibrated to reproduce reionization observable. The filamentary networks were detected and then extracted using an open-source code called DisPerSE. Notwithstanding the complexity inherent in the process of isolating the effect of background radiation on baryonic filaments, some trends did stand out. Thes trends vary based on the baryonic density of the filament region being talked about -- since all the filaments segments were combined, and later categoized in density bins.

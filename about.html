About Me

I was born in a town called Jorhat located in the northeastern state of Assam in India. Despite having lived most of my formative years in Guwahati, a larger (and noisier) city in Assam, my roots remain quietly anchored in Jorhat — a place of slower days, the earthy scent of rain-soaked soil lingering after heavy downpours, and memories of times spent with grandparents, visceral Śākta ceremonies, and sunlit cricket matches with cousins in the courtyard.

My academic journey couldn’t have been more different. Anyone who ends up becoming an astronomer or astrophysicist can almost always trace it back to a childhood memory of being taken to the city planetarium by a parent — and mine was no exception. My father had me memorizing the names of the planets in our solar system when I was barely five, handed me A Brief History of Time when I was thirteen, and spent countless nights diving into astronomy chapters from encyclopaedias with me.
